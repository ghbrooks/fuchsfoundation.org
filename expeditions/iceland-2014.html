The Chris Henstock Memorial Expedition 2014

The Fuchs Foundation Expedition Challenges for Teachers

The Fuchs Foundation has been running expeditions for teachers since 2005. Expeditions last 4-6 weeks and take place in polar regions, focused on teamwork and scientific work.

In 2013 the Foundation expanded to include all education sector teachers and students from 6th form through higher education. Shorter expeditions to varied destinations are now offered.

This expedition honours the memory of Chris Henstock, education advisor to the Fuchs Foundation, who was a great inspiration.

Route

Trek to and across the Drangajokull icecap on the North West peninsula, with wild camping in the high mountains. Route: Keflavik to Reykjavik to Isafjordur, ferry to the North West peninsula. 5 days trek duration.

Cost

£1600 per person. Includes: training, leaders, flights, food, equipment, insurance, backup support. Excluded: UK travel, personal equipment, baggage/clothing insurance.

Eligibility

Practising teachers in all sectors of education and students in 6th form, further or higher education.

Application

Deposit of £100 required with application form.

Training

Easter holidays 2014 in the Peak District weekend covering campcraft, equipment, fitness, fundraising, and projects.
